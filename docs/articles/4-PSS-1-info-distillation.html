Allow Me to Service this Questionnaire Professionally #1 - Information Distillation

Uploaded on 2025 June 7th

I've been wanting to write an article with this title for a while - mostly because Information Distillation sounds cool, I admit. It also sounds arcane a bit, especially if you say Knowledge Distillation instead, like I'm an alchemist trying to create a Philosopher's Stone which will be used along with a couple of dozen brains to create the Juice of KnowledgeTM. Yum.

The first part, the one that makes this sound like this article is the first in a series of articles comes from the fact that at the moment I'm part of the "Professional Services" division at my company. When I get to take my first look at a questionnaire, it's already been deemed "Ready for Professional Services" and assigned to me. So because I'm approx. five years old, I always think:

That is me.
I am Professional Services.
Allow me to service this questionnaire professionally.

Hehehe

Now, this article is supposed to be about Information Distillation - what do I mean by that? I mean that in the above-mentioned questionnaires I sometimes encounter situations or instructions that seem a bit overcomplicated, too wordy. Some are justifiably complicated, because they're describing a complicated situation. Other times I get the feeling that multiple people worked on the same questionnaire, but not necessarily at the same time or with the same information, or with not enough time to get to know the questionnaire.

To better illustrate what I mean, you can find a short excerpt from an imaginary study below.

  • ...

  • QGEN. What do you identify as?
    Select one

  • QCHILD. Do you have children?
    Select one

  • ...

What happened in this imaginary study is that new QGEN options kept being added, and they kept being added to the instructions at QCHILD as well. You can note that the above instructions are still correct, but they can be significantly shortened if we switch to a positive affirmation:
P.N. Don't Show only for QGEN option 2

I realise that this might seem like a trivial improvement to make, but I did it to make a point - it had to be obvious. I would just like it if more people took a minute or two (if possible :P) when looking over instructions in order to think a bit and ease the burden on their minds and on the minds of anyone who comes after. :)
